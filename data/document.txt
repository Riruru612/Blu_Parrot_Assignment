Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines.
These machines are designed to perform tasks such as reasoning, learning, and problem-solving.
AI systems can analyze data, recognize patterns, and make decisions with minimal human intervention.
Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence.
It focuses on enabling systems to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.
Machine learning algorithms improve their performance as they are exposed to more data.
Deep learning is a specialized field within machine learning.
It uses neural networks with multiple layers to process complex data such as images and text.
Deep learning models are widely used in computer vision and natural language processing tasks.
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of AI that deals with understanding human language.
It enables machines to read, interpret, and generate human language.
Applications of NLP include chatbots, translation systems, and sentiment analysis.
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines information retrieval with text generation.
Instead of relying only on the model's internal knowledge, RAG retrieves relevant information from external sources.
This improves accuracy and reduces hallucination in responses.
A RAG system consists of two main components: a retriever and a generator.
The retriever searches for relevant documents or text chunks.
The generator uses the retrieved information to produce a final answer.
Embeddings are numerical representations of text.
They convert words or sentences into vectors in a high-dimensional space.
These vectors allow machines to measure similarity between different pieces of text.
FAISS (Facebook AI Similarity Search) is a library used for efficient similarity search.
It helps in storing and retrieving vector embeddings quickly.
FAISS is widely used in building vector databases for AI applications.
Chunking is the process of dividing large text into smaller pieces.
This helps in better retrieval and processing of information.
Proper chunking improves the performance of retrieval systems.
Prompt engineering is the practice of designing inputs for language models.
A well-designed prompt ensures that the model generates accurate and relevant responses.
It is especially important in RAG systems to guide the model properly.
One limitation of RAG systems is that they depend heavily on the quality of retrieved documents.
If irrelevant chunks are retrieved, the final answer may be incorrect.
Another limitation is that basic RAG systems do not perform reasoning beyond retrieved data.
Despite these limitations, RAG systems are widely used in applications such as question answering, document search, and chatbots.
They provide a balance between retrieval accuracy and generative capabilities.
